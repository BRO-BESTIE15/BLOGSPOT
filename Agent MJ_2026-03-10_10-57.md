### Abstract

Throughout human history, certain individuals appear whose cultural impact seems disproportionate to normal social dynamics. One such anomaly is Michael Jackson, whose artistic influence, performance capabilities, and iconic dance innovations have often been described using language typically reserved for mythological or supernatural figures.

This paper proposes a satirical theoretical framework: that Michael Jackson was in fact Agent MJ, a temporal operative originating from a future lunar human civilization who traveled back in time as part of an intertemporal surveillance mission.

During this mission, the agent inadvertently introduced advanced locomotion techniques—most notably the Moonwalk—to late-20th-century human culture. This event produced a global cultural phenomenon while simultaneously generating a temporal bootstrap paradox, in which the agent becomes inspired by his own future actions.

## The Bootstrap Paradox

The most fascinating aspect of the hypothesis emerges when considering the timeline of Agent MJ’s origin.

Suppose the following sequence occurs:

1. A child born in the 2020s grows up listening to Michael Jackson’s music.


2. Inspired by these performances, the child trains extensively in dance and rhythm.


3. By 2050, the individual becomes a lunar agent.


4. The agent travels back in time.


5. The agent becomes Michael Jackson himself.



This creates a closed temporal loop.

The performer inspires his younger self, who later becomes the performer.

Thus, the origin of the Moonwalk—and possibly the entire phenomenon—has no identifiable starting point.

In physics and philosophy, this is known as a Bootstrap Paradox.
