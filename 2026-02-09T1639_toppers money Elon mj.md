📌 Toppers vs Above-Average: Two Different Lives

In school, I always noticed two types of students.

One: Toppers.
Always aiming for 80/80, 95%, rank 1.

Second: Above-average students like me.
We aim for 60–70%, sometimes 80%+, and feel happy if our effort matches our result.

I’ve seen toppers cry when their marks drop a little.
80/80 → 65/80 = breakdown.

For them, marks = self-worth.

For me: “Jo effort diya, wahi mila. I’m okay.”

And honestly, that feels more peaceful.


---

📌 The Dopamine Ventilator Trap

Many toppers live on results.

Good marks → dopamine high 😄
Low marks → emotional crash 😭

It becomes an addiction.

They don’t study for learning.
They study for validation.

Slowly, burnout comes.

Anxiety comes.

And peace goes away.

Success in system? Yes.
Success in life? Not always.

Rich and successful people often say:

“Money can’t buy happiness.”

Freedom ✔️

But for normal people?

Money = less stress.

Money = options.

That matters.
