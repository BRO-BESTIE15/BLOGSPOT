The Teacher Who Made Me Stop Asking

I don't think I ever hated mathematics.

I think I started hating my mathematics classes.

From Class 8 to Class 10, I had the same maths teacher. And for those three years, a pattern kept repeating itself.

I would get stuck on a concept.

I would raise my hand and ask a doubt.

She would explain it again.

I still wouldn't understand.

So I would ask again.

And again.

And after some point, instead of another explanation, I would hear things like:

"You don't want to understand."

That sentence always annoyed me.

Because if I didn't want to understand, why was I still asking?

Why would I keep trying to figure it out?

Why would I spend time on the same problem instead of just giving up?

The funny part is that many times I would come home, open YouTube, watch a lecture, and suddenly understand the exact same concept that confused me in class.

So was the problem really that I didn't want to understand?

Or was the explanation simply not working for me?

This happened so many times that eventually I stopped asking doubts to her.

Not because I was scared.

I was irritated.

I was tired of asking the same question, not understanding the answer, and then being treated as if the problem was my attitude.

Nobody enjoys feeling disrespected in front of an entire class.

Then came the irony.

In Class 10, my performance in some maths tests was terrible. Absolute disaster-class performance.

And one day she pointed at some topper who regularly asked doubts and scored well and told me that I wasn't performing because I didn't ask doubts.

I remember thinking:

"Do you know why I stopped asking doubts in the first place?"

Because every time I did, it felt like I was being blamed for not understanding.

The situation got worse over time.

There was one argument that became much bigger than the others. Personal comments were made. I got triggered. An argument started.

And from that day, I think I genuinely started disliking her.

Maybe for her I was just one student among thousands she has taught throughout her career.

But for me, she was my maths teacher for several important years of my life.

That's the difference.

Teachers meet thousands of students.

Students only get a handful of teachers.

A teacher can forget an interaction in a week.

A student can remember it for years.

Even today, what frustrates me most isn't that I struggled with maths.

It's being told that I didn't want to understand when I was literally trying to understand.

The evidence was right there.

I kept asking.

I kept trying.

And when school explanations didn't work, I went home and learned from YouTube, friends, and anywhere else I could find help.

That doesn't sound like someone who didn't want to understand.

It sounds like someone who was trying to understand but needed a different explanation.

Maybe that's why this still stays in my mind.

Not because of mathematics.

But because of how easy it is for a struggling student to be mistaken for a careless one.
