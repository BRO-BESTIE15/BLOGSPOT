Kabir's Views, My Values, and Teacher's Excuse

Today in Hindi period we completed the chapter about Kabir. After finishing it, sir gave us an out-of-syllabus question:

"Kabir ke jeevan aur unke pad se hame kya shiksha milti hai?"

Everybody started writing like it was the most normal question ever.

Meanwhile my brain:

"Wait... what if someone doesn't agree with Kabir's views? Why should everyone write five points about what they learned from him?" 💀

I wasn't saying Kabir is wrong. I just thought the question was subjective. Reading someone's writings and personally accepting their teachings are two different things.

My friend, being the great source of bad decisions, whispered that I should ask the teacher.

So I did.

Worst timing ever. ☠️

Instead of getting a simple answer, I somehow unlocked a full lecture. It slowly shifted from Kabir's teachings to life, character, values, and then somehow my own views entered the conversation. I just stood there thinking, "How did we get here?"

I stayed silent because I didn't want to turn Hindi period into a debate.

The funny thing is that I personally didn't feel like I learned anything new from Kabir. Most of the ideas discussed were things I had already thought about in my own way. The only difference is that Kabir wrote them in Hindi that takes me a whole day to decode. 😭

Maybe the question was supposed to mean, "What message does Kabir try to give?" But my brain interpreted it as, "What should I personally learn from him?" And that's where everything went off the rails.

Anyway...

Next time my friend tells me, "Bro, ask the question."

I'm pretending I didn't hear him. 💀
